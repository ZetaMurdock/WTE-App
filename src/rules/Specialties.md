# Specialties

Specialties represent hyper-specific training domains. They have a pool of **200 points** to allocate at creation, with a maximum of **75 points** in any single specialty. 

Specialty checks are resolved using a **1d40** roll:
$$\text{Roll} = 1d40 + \text{Specialty Points} + \text{Attribute Modifier}$$

A specialty with **under 25 points** is untrained: its checks take a flat **−25 penalty** (this applies to Pressure Engine skill rolls too).

---

### The Specialties List

*   **Inspiration:** Mental creativity, flash insights, and overcoming cognitive hurdles.
*   **Balance:** Staying centered under pressure, physical stabilization, and poise.
*   **Weight:** Kinetic force control, heavy impact management, and leverage.
*   **Precision:** Target acquisition, lockpicking, micro-mechanics, and critical hits.
*   **Control:** Emotional restraint, driving, and precise device piloting.
*   **Wpn Mastery (Weapon Mastery):** Direct damage output and combat maneuvers.
*   **Mental Fort. (Mental Fortitude):** Resisting psychological shock, stress, and Eldritch decay.
*   **Perception:** Notice hidden elements, secret doors, traps, and tracks.
*   **Adaption (Adaptation):** Surviving hazard shifts, high radiation, or void exposure.
*   **Cunning:** Stealth, deception, infiltration, and exploiting weaknesses.

---

### The Tax Burden (Derived Reductions)
Every specialty point allocates training focus away from basic physiological reserves. For every **3 points** in a specialty, a specific [Derived Statistic](wte://rules/Derived_Statistics) is reduced by **1 point**. Building an unbalanced specialist can reduce essential derived attributes (like HP or Movement) to dangerous or even negative values.
