# Negotiation & Diplomacy

W.T.E is not a combat-only system. A contract with a high-profile client, a
standoff you talk your way out of, or a Paradigm hearing is resolved with the
**Negotiation Engine** — the Pressure Engine's chassis aimed at a *person*
instead of a situation.

---

### The Track

Every counterpart has a **Resistance** value. You are trying to drive it to zero.

| Resistance | Room |
|---|---|
| 0 | **Accord** — the deal is yours |
| 1–20 | Receptive |
| 21–50 | Guarded |
| 51–149 | Resistant |
| 150 | **Sealed** — they are done listening |

---

### Resolving an Exchange

Pick **1–4 approaches** — the specialties you are actually leaning on:

*   **Cunning** — leverage, reading the angle, what they don't want said aloud
*   **Control** — holding composure when they push
*   **Perception** — catching the tell, the pause, the thing left out
*   **Inspiration** — a genuinely novel offer they hadn't considered
*   **Mental Fortitude** — withstanding a hostile or predatory counterpart
*   **Adaptation / Balance / Precision** — matching their register, timing, exact terms

Each approach rolls:
$$(1d40 + \text{Specialty Pts} + \text{Attribute Mod} + \text{Complexity}) \times \text{Rank Multiplier}$$

Your **AAV** is the average of those rolls, plus a cohesion bonus (3 approaches
→ +1, 4 → +2), **plus your Influence**, **minus any standing gap**. Compare
AAV to their Resistance; the difference lands in the standard outcome bands.
A success drives Resistance *down*; a fumble hardens it.

---

### Influence — the Social Attack Power

**Influence** (Charisma + Cunning + Perception + Precision, reduced by
Adaptation and Weight) adds directly to every exchange. It is to diplomacy what
Attack Power is to a fight.

Note the deliberate cost: **Charisma reduces Perception Range**. The best talker
in the party is the *worst* at reading the room — diplomacy is meant to be run
by a pair, a face and a watcher.

**The Hollow Signature:** a character on the Process pole of the
[Polarized Soul](wte://rules/The_Polarized_Soul_System) has Influence 0 and
cannot use Complexity. They are not persuasive; they are correct. That is a
build choice with teeth.

---

### Eminence — the Standing Gate

A high-profile client has an **Eminence requirement**. If your Eminence (the
System Alignment Index, −20 … +20) falls short, the shortfall is subtracted
from your AAV before a die is read — a known liability walks into the
Directorate already losing.

Species carry an Eminence Nature that colours every first impression: a Seraph
(Civilized +40) is trusted by default; a Stygian (Feral +20) is flagged before
they speak. Winning a hard negotiation is one of the cleanest ways to *earn*
Eminence back.

---

### Curator Guidance

*   Set Resistance by how much the counterpart has to lose — a dock foreman is
    20–30; a Paradigm Director is 90+.
*   Failure should **escalate, not end**. A hardened track is a scene that got
    worse, not a closed door.
*   Let the table spend real resources: a favour, an item, intel, or an
    Eminence hit to force an angle.
*   Reward the pairing. If one player talks and another reads the room, give the
    reader's Perception result to the talker as an extra approach.
